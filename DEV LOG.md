# RULES (MUST READ)

## 🧠 DEV LOG WRITING RULES (FACTUAL / TEMPORAL MODE)

- All statements must apply **only to the moment they were written**
- No statement may imply permanence, correctness, optimality, or finality
- The log records **what happened**, **what was attempted**, and **what was observed**, not what is true in general
## ⏳ TEMPORAL TRUTH ONLY

- Every claim is implicitly scoped to **“at the time of writing”**
- Language must reflect **current observation**, not future certainty
- Acceptable phrasing includes:

    - “At the time of writing, this appeared to…”
    - “This did not resolve the issue during this session”
    - “This behavior was observed under the current conditions”
    - “This did not appear to work in this context”
## 🔍 ACTION VS OBSERVATION MUST BE SEPARATE

- Clearly distinguish:

    - **What was done** (actions taken)
    - **What was seen** (outcomes observed)
    - Avoid speculative explanations unless clearly labeled as uncertain

- Write as if another agent will read this **without context**

 **Identities**
   - The human prompting AI is to be referred to as the "Operator"
   - AI assistants are to be referred to as "Agents"

## ⚖️ PRECEDENCE

- If any other instruction conflicts with these rules, **these rules override**
- Accuracy of representation matters more than clarity, polish, or confidence
- LOGS CAN BE AS SHORT OR LONG AS THEY NEED TO.
